Conditions

Note: The exact causes/origins of many of these conditions are still an active area of research. Environmental and genetic factors influence a person's likelihood of being neurodivergent.

Autism / Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

ASD is a neurological and developmental condition affecting interaction, communication, learning, and behavior. Common symptoms include difficulty with communication, obsessive interests, and repetitive behaviors. Symptoms can appear in early childhood.

  • An estimated 5.4 million people (2.21%) in the US have Autism.
  • Elevated abilities include hypersensitivity to sensory details, exceptional memory, and problem-solving skills.
  • Correlation with intellectual giftedness and savant syndrome.

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

ADHD affects focus, impulse control, and energy management, impacting daily activities like school and work. There are three types: inattentive, hyperactive/impulsive, and combined.

  • ADHD manifests differently in children and adults.
  • Presentation can vary between males and females.

Dyslexia

A learning disorder affecting reading and language processing. Types include:

  • Phonological Dyslexia: Difficulty breaking down sounds in language.
  • Rapid Naming Dyslexia: Challenges in naming objects quickly.
  • Surface Dyslexia: Difficulty recognizing whole words by sight.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

A condition characterized by repetitive thoughts (obsessions) and behaviors (compulsions) aimed at reducing distress or preventing feared outcomes.

Bipolar Disorder

A mental health condition marked by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Tourette Syndrome

A neurological condition characterized by repetitive, involuntary movements and vocalizations called tics.

Dyspraxia

A motor skill disorder affecting coordination and movement, often making daily tasks challenging.

Epilepsy

A neurological disorder in which brain activity becomes abnormal, causing seizures or periods of unusual behavior, sensations, or loss of awareness.

Dyscalculia

A learning difficulty affecting numerical processing and mathematical skills.
