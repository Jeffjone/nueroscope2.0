Terminology

Neurodiversity

Neurodiversity is the concept that variations in brain function and behavior are normal and natural aspects of human diversity. People fundamentally experience and interact with the world in different ways to varying degrees and there is no single, "correct" way of thinking, learning, and behaving. There are neurological differences that can negatively impact a person's ability to navigate the world we live in. As we understand more about how people's experience differ, we can adapt our actions and our world to make sure every person's experience is accounted for.

Neurotypical

Refers to an individual who thinks, perceives, and behaves in ways that are statistically and/or socially considered the norm by the general population.

Neurodiverse

Refers to an individual whose brain works in a way that isn’t expected or isn’t considered to be the norm; the individual functions differently in terms of learning, social interaction, and communication.

Neurodevelopmental Conditions

These are conditions that affect the development of the nervous system, including the brain. They can cause the body to exhibit growth differently, impacting communication, learning, and social interaction. Alternative methods for learning and communication are often necessary to support growth.

Stimming

"Stimming" refers to self-stimulatory behaviors, such as repetitive movements or sounds. These behaviors help manage sensory input and regulate emotions, often acting as a self-soothing mechanism for individuals.

Restricted Repetitive Behaviors

These behaviors include fixations on specific patterns, strict adherence to routines, or repetitive movements. They vary greatly in type and severity among individuals.

Hyperfocus

The ability to concentrate intensely on one task while ignoring others. It is a type of automatic attention that requires less effort to maintain.

Special Interests

Highly focused interests in specific topics or hobbies, which may be lifelong or change over time. Examples include characters, music, books, or paintings.
